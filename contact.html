How to get referred

Most people are referred automatically after a heart attack, surgery, or procedure. If you were not referred, or if you were not sure whether it was offered to you, here is what to do:

Option 1 — Ask your GP

Your GP can refer you to the local cardiac rehab programme. Just mention that you had a heart event and would like to be referred. Your GP can also tell you whether you are eligible.

Option 2 — Ask your cardiac nurse or consultant

If you are still in contact with the hospital team, ask your cardiac nurse, physiologist, or consultant cardiologist to refer you. They can do this directly.

Option 3 — Contact us directly

In some cases you may be able to refer yourself. Call or email the team using the details above and tell us about your diagnosis. We will let you know what information we need.

What happens after referral?

Once referred, you will be contacted by the team to arrange an initial assessment appointment. At this appointment they will assess your fitness, review your medical history, and design your personal programme. This usually happens within a few weeks of referral.
