About the Programme

What it is, what to expect, and why it works — explained clearly.

What is the programme?

This is a free NHS programme that helps you recover and stay well after a heart event. It combines supervised exercise, education about your heart, and emotional support — all in one place.

It is run by a team of specialists — including physiotherapists, nurses, dietitians, and psychologists — who work together to support your recovery. You do not have to face this alone.

Who is it for?

CR is suitable for people who have had:

  • A heart attack (myocardial infarction)
  • A coronary artery bypass graft (CABG)
  • A stent or angioplasty (PCI)
  • Heart failure
  • Heart valve surgery
  • Stable angina
  • Certain other cardiac conditions — ask your team

Age, fitness level, weight, and smoking status are not barriers to joining. The programme is designed to meet you where you are right now.

The four stages of CR

1

Stage 1 — In hospital

While you are still in hospital after your heart event, a CR team member may visit you. They will give you basic information about your condition and what to expect when you go home.

2

Stage 2 — Early recovery at home

In the first few weeks at home, you are encouraged to rest and gradually increase gentle activity. You may receive a home visit or phone call from the CR team. This is a normal and important part of recovery.

3

Stage 3 — Outpatient CR programme

This is the main programme — usually 6 to 8 weeks of group sessions at a local venue. Sessions typically include supervised exercise, health talks, and time to ask questions. Most people attend once or twice a week. This is free on the NHS.

4

Stage 4 — Long-term maintenance

After completing Stage 3, you will be supported to continue being active independently. This might include community exercise groups, walking programmes, or home-based activity plans.

What to expect at a Stage 3 session

⏳

How long?

Sessions usually last around 1 to 1.5 hours, including a warm-up, main exercise, and cool-down.

👥

Group or individual?

Most sessions are small groups, but your exercise programme is personal to you. You work at your own level.

📋

What happens?

Warm-up exercises, aerobic activity (walking, cycling, circuits), cool-down, and often a short talk on a health topic.

🩹

Is it safe?

Yes. Your blood pressure and heart rate are monitored. The team is trained to respond to any situation that arises.

💕

What to bring?

Comfortable clothing and trainers, any medications you take, and a bottle of water. That is all you need.

💬

Emotional support

Feeling anxious or low is normal. The team and other participants understand — you are not alone in how you feel.
