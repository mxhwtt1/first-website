Post-Activation Potentiation (PAP)

Fact

Heavy pre-activation exercises can increase muscular power output

Wilson, J.M., Duncan, N.M., Marin, P.J., Brown, L.E., Loenneke, J.P., Wilson, S.M.C., Jo, E., Lowery, R.P. and Ugrinowitsch, C. (2013) 'META-ANALYSIS OF POSTACTIVATION POTENTIATION AND POWER: EFFECTS OF CONDITIONING ACTIVITY, VOLUME, GENDER, REST PERIODS, AND TRAINING STATUS', The Journal of Strength & Conditioning Research, 27(3), pp. 853–859.
Fact

PAP has less effect on untrained athletes

Chiu, L.Z.F., Fry, A.C., Weiss, L.W., Schilling, B.K., Brown, L.E. and Smith, S.L. Postactivation Potentiation Response in Athletic and Recreationally Trained Individuals. Also: Wilson, J.M. et al. (2013) The Journal of Strength & Conditioning Research, 27(3), pp. 853–859.
Fact

The optimal rest time for PAP effects is between 4–12 minutes

Kilduff, L.P., Owen, N., Bevan, H., Bennett, M., Kingsley, M.I.C. and Cunningham, D. (2008) Influence of recovery time on post-activation potentiation in professional rugby players, pp. 795. Also: Chen, Y., Su, Q., Yang, J., Li, G., Zhang, S., Lv, Y. and Yu, L. (2023) Effects of rest interval and training intensity on jumping performance: a systematic review and meta-analysis investigating post-activation performance enhancement. Frontiers Media SA.
Fact

Training status, intensity, and rest period length influence PAP performance improvements

Tillin, N.A. and Bishop, D. (2012) Factors Modulating Post-Activation Potentiation and its Effect on Performance of Subsequent Explosive Activities.
Fact

PAP increases neural activation of the muscles

Blazevich, A.J. and Babault, N. (2019) Post-activation Potentiation Versus Post-activation Performance Enhancement in Humans: Historical Perspective, Underlying Mechanisms, and Current Issues. Frontiers Media SA.
Fact

PAP protocols often include high intensity strength exercises of 80% 1RM or more

Seitz, L.B. and Haff, G.G. (2015) Factors Modulating Post-Activation Potentiation of Jump, Sprint, Throw, and Upper-Body Ballistic Performances: A Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis. Springer Science and Business Media LLC, pp. 231.
Fact

PAP performance effects depend on the balance between fatigue and potentiation

Tillin, N.A. and Bishop, D. (2012) Factors Modulating Post-Activation Potentiation and its Effect on Performance of Subsequent Explosive Activities.

Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation (PNF)

Fact

PNF stretching performed immediately before exercise may temporarily reduce strength or power performance

Behm, D.G., Blazevich, A.J., Kay, A.D. and Mchugh, M. (2015) Acute effects of muscle stretching on physical performance, range of motion, and injury incidence in healthy active individuals: a systematic review. Canadian Science Publishing, pp. 1.
Fact

One PNF stretching session can significantly increase joint range of motion temporarily

Konrad, A. and Tilp, M. (2014) Increased range of motion after static stretching is not due to changes in muscle and tendon structures. Elsevier BV, pp. 636.
Fact

Flexibility improvements from PNF are partly due to increased tolerance to stretch sensations

Magnusson, S.P., Simonsen, E.B., Aagaard, P., Dyhre-Poulsen, P., McHugh, M.P., Kjaer, M. and McCombe Waller, S. (1996) 'Mechanical and physical responses to stretching with and without preisometric contraction in human skeletal muscle', Archives of physical medicine and rehabilitation, 77(4), pp. 373–378.
Fact

PNF is widely used in sports training and physiotherapy to improve flexibility and mobility

Hindle, K.B., Whitcomb, T.J., Briggs, W.O. and Hong, J. (2012) Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation (PNF): Its Mechanisms and Effects on Range of Motion and Muscular Function. Termedia Sp. z.o.o., pp. 105.
Fact

PNF stretching works partly because Golgi tendon organs reduce muscle activation

Hindle, K.B., Whitcomb, T.J., Briggs, W.O. and Hong, J. (2012) Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation (PNF): Its Mechanisms and Effects on Range of Motion and Muscular Function. Termedia Sp. z.o.o., pp. 105.

Electrical Muscle Stimulation (EMS)

Fact

External electrical impulses trigger muscle contractions by depolarising motor neurons

Milosevic, M., Marquez-Chin, C., Masani, K., Hirata, M., Nomura, T., Popovic, M.R. and Nakazawa, K. (2020) Why brain-controlled neuroprosthetics matter: mechanisms underlying electrical stimulation of muscles and nerves in rehabilitation. Springer Science and Business Media LLC.
Fact

EMS training can increase muscle strength

Maffiuletti, N.A. and Maffiuletti, N.T.N.A. (2010) Physiological and methodological considerations for the use of neuromuscular electrical stimulation. Springer Science and Business Media LLC, pp. 223.
Fact

EMS can recruit fast-twitch muscle fibres that are difficult to activate during voluntary exercise

Gregory, C.M. and Bickel, C.S. (2005) 'Recruitment Patterns in Human Skeletal Muscle During Electrical Stimulation', Physical Therapy, 85(4), pp. 358–364.
Fact

EMS training can improve strength and power performance in athletes

Filipovic, A., Kleinöder, H., Dörmann, U. and Mester, J. ELECTROMYOSTIMULATION — A SYSTEMATIC REVIEW OF THE EFFECTS OF DIFFERENT ELECTROMYOSTIMULATION METHODS ON SELECTED STRENGTH PARAMETERS IN TRAINED AND ELITE ATHLETES.
Fact

EMS can activate large motor units earlier than voluntary contractions

Yoo, H., Park, S., Oh, S., Kang, M., Seo, Y., Kim, B.G. and Lee, S. (2023) Effects of electrical muscle stimulation on core muscle activation and physical performance in non-athletic adults: A randomized controlled trial. Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health).
